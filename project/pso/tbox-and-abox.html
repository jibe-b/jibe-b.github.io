TBox and ABox

 TBox

Terminology is first the result of the "recensement"(fr) of concepts (Classes in OWL) and relationships (Object Properties).

Described Classes (classes that only admits for subclasses, classes that correspond to a description) and Property Chains enable an OWL-DL reasoner to infer and make explicit any implicit relationship.

ABox

Knowledge propositions (like Models, Hypothesis, Theorems) are referenced as Named Graphs and have an equivalent Individual in the ontology.

Any relationship that is obtains using a SWRL reasoner is written into the ontology.

The development of the ontology is therefore done in parallel with the development of an RDF graph.

This graph has three levels:

  • first level is editorial-oriented, that is to say it talks about what can be found in a scientific article (article metadata, article structure),
  • second-level is theory-oriented, i.e. talks about models, hypothesis, results and reasoning
  • third-level is domain-oriented: every triple is about Physics, usually the explicit form of a Physical Sciences statememt.
