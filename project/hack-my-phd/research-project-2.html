Research project

Main idea

Biochemical networks are higly non-linear and their evolution inform on fundamental characteristics aquisition in particular for the problem of drug resistance aquisiton.

Available databases include biochemical network datasets and phyllogenetic trees.

Combining both will enable to study biochemical networks both on the statics and evolutionary approches.

What are the models that reproduce the aquisition of complex biochemical networks through evolution?

In silico evolution models are available and will be studied.

Analysis of the models needed to interprete and reproduce the observed trajectories will be studied, in order to identify lack of experimental data and flaws of simulation models and provide experiments and theoretical models demands.

This general process will be applied to a set of biochemical processes for which:

  • phyllogenitic relationships are available for a set of closely related species
  • data on the biochemical process and optionaly the expression levels (raw data) is available
  • display non-linear behaviour
